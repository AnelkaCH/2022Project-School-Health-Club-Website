# Health Really Matters ❤️‍🩹

(Note: This project was made in 2022, but I decided to upload it to GitHub in 2026 to keep it safe :D)

This was a health-awareness website developed in 2022 as part of a school Biology project and later showcased at my school's STEAM Fair.

During the COVID-19 pandemic, misinformation about health and disease was widespread online. This project was created to provide accessible health information from reputable medical sources and encourage users to learn more about important health topics. In addition to COVID-19 educational content, the website also covers other medical conditions, such as arrhythmia, to promote broader health awareness.

## Live Demo 🌐

https://health-really-matters.netlify.app/

## Project Overview 🤔

'Health Really Matters' was a made-up school club that would hypothetically focus around healthy lifestyles. We wanted to accompany the club with a website. My team members researched and gathered information from trusted medical sources, while I was responsible for designing, developing, and deploying the website using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

The website was later displayed at our school's STEAM Fair as part of the project presentation.

## Objectives ☑️

* Provide clear and accessible information about COVID-19.
* Help users find trustworthy health resources.
* Raise awareness of other medical conditions, including arrhythmia.
* Present educational content through an engaging and easy-to-navigate website.

## Features ⚙️

* Educational content related to COVID-19.
* Information about other health conditions.
* References to reputable medical sources.
* Multi-page website structure.
* Responsive front-end design.

## Sources and Research 📰

Information presented on the website was gathered from reputable medical and public health organizations, including:

* Mayo Clinic
* National Institutes of Health (NIH)
* Other established healthcare and educational sources

The project's goal was to encourage users to rely on credible information rather than unverified claims commonly found online during the pandemic.
