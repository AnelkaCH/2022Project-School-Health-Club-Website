Overview

An arrhythmia is a problem with the rate or rhythm of your heartbeat. Your heart may beat too quickly or too slowly, or even with an irregular rhythm. It is normal for your heart rate to speed up or slow down depending on the intensity of the activity. However, a frequent irregular rhythm may mean that your heart is not pumping enough blood to your body. A fast heart is called tachycardia, while a slow heart is called bradycardia.
