Is Covid on the rise in the U.S.?

By Anelka Cornelius Hariyanto | 09/09/2023

There are lots of signs in the U.S. that point to a rise in Covid activity as the season of autumn approaches.

Hospitalizations for Covid patients are on the rise, after hitting an all-time low in late June. According to the Our World In Data, in August 31, the most recent date for which data is available, there were just over 19,200 people hospitalized with Covid, up nearly 13% from the previous week. Deaths from Covid increased by nearly 18% over the previous week.

And with students going back to school in mid-September, there could be a high chance for a rise in Covid cases.

“Every single one of those things is showing us that we have increased rates of Covid transmission in our communities,” said Jodie Guest, a professor of epidemiology at Emory University’s Rollins School of Public Health in Atlanta.

Is the variant EG.5 to blame?

EG.5, or commonly known as Eris, is continuing to spread quickly, and has become a dominant variant not only in the U.S., but also in the world. This variant is spreading rapidly across the world because it has one notable mutation that helps it evade the antibodies created by our immune system in response to the earlier vaccines and variants.

However, cases of this variant seem to be considerably mild and the new vaccine formulation that is expected to be rolled out in the U.S. later in September targets the variant XBB.1.5, which is genetically similar to EG.5. In theory, this will be effective against this variant.

How do we protect ourselves from getting infected?

The steps to be safe from Covid are still the same: wear your mask, reguarally wash your hands, stay away from crowded areas, stay at home if ill, take a test if symptomatic, and be vaccinated. The CDC noted that improving ventilation indoors, like opening windows, turning on fans and using air filters to clean the air, can help protect against infection.
